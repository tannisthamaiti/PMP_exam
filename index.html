The Project Management Plan is the container — its component plans each govern one aspect of the project. The Scope Baseline is the reference: work added outside the CCB is scope creep. The Cost Baseline (activity costs + contingency reserves) sits inside the overall project budget.
Change request received
From a stakeholder, sponsor, or team member
↓
Review & assess impact on the project
Always the first step — understand impact on scope, time, cost & risk before any action
↓
Submit to the Change Control Board (CCB)
Only the CCB can officially approve or reject changes
↓
CCB decision
Approved or rejected?
↓
Approved
Execute in Direct & Manage Project Work
Only this process executes approved changes — not Monitor & Control or Validate Scope
Rejected
Notify the requesting stakeholder
Inform them the request was not approved by the CCB
↓
Update the Change Log
Record every decision — both approvals and rejections
Exam tip: the first action is always to review & assess — never stop work, never execute before understanding the impact. When a deliverable is accepted by the sponsor → move to Close Project or Phase. If the deliverable is NOT accepted yet → the project cannot close.

Earned value formulas — equation · calculation · interpretation
Term Full name Formula Worked example Positive / >1 means Negative / <1 means
Base values — the four inputs every EV formula needs
BAC Budget at Completion = total planned budget $90,000 The finish line — fixed at planning
PV Planned Value = budgeted cost of work scheduled $16,000 What you planned to spend by now
EV Earned Value = BAC × % complete $14,000 Dollar value of work actually completed
AC Actual Cost = amount actually spent $30,000 What you have actually spent so far
Variance formulas — positive result is good
CV Cost Variance EV − AC 32k − 30k = +$2,000 + Under budget − Over budget
SV Schedule Variance EV − PV 14k − 16k = −$2,000 + Ahead of schedule − Behind schedule
Performance indices — greater than 1 is good
CPI Cost Performance Index EV ÷ AC 32k ÷ 30k = 1.07
0.9 CPI = 10% over budget		 >1 Under budget <1 Over budget
SPI Schedule Performance Index EV ÷ PV 14k ÷ 16k = 0.875 >1 Ahead of schedule <1 Behind schedule
Forecast formulas — predicting the final cost
EAC Estimate at Completion BAC ÷ CPI 90k ÷ 1.06 = $84,906 Forecast total project cost at finish
ETC Estimate to Complete EAC − AC 84,906 − 30k = $54,906 Remaining cost needed to finish
VAC Variance at Completion BAC − EAC 90k − 84,906 = +$5,094 + Will finish under budget − Will finish over budget
TCPI To Complete Performance Index (BAC − EV) ÷ (BAC − AC) (90k−32k) ÷ (90k−30k)
= 58k ÷ 60k = 0.97		 <1 Achievable — easier than current pace >1 Must improve efficiency to hit BAC
Reading CPI: a CPI of 0.9 means for every $1 spent, only $0.90 of work is delivered — the project is 10% over budget. EAC = BAC ÷ CPI is the standard exam formula (divide total budget by current efficiency). Management Reserves are for unknown unknowns and are NOT in the cost baseline — only the project budget includes them. The cost baseline displayed as an S-curve graph is a time-phased view of planned spending.
